Review

The main story is okay, not bad, but not a must read either. We follow as of currently a cast of 2 human and 4 T-doll. The Commander, Mayling the Engineer, Groza the T-Doll leader, Krolik a T-Doll with an agressive personality, Nemesis a T-Doll who speaks words that only Krolik can decipher, and finally Colphne a Medical T-Doll as they trek through the wasteland attempting to uncover the secret of the package they have to deliver.

To me personally the current six chapter that are out do not fully entice me into the world as of yet. Now the question of will I continue playing "till it get good" is yet to be determine. What I will give the game credit for are the nice 3D cut scene they have for some of the mission. Those scene are very high quailty and well done in themselves.

I will not go too much into the combat as that is subjective, as shown in the combat section of this page, the game features a xcom style turn base system, there's no chance of any attack missing but damage can be reduce if enemies are behind cover. Whatever a person will like the gameplay loop or not will be completely up to them. If they do not like the this combat system, they'll probably not enjoy the game overall since, every gamemode is basically this with the execption of limited time event specific gamemode.

Now let's talk about the T-Doll, specifically character design. The design of the T-Dolls themselves are rather simplistic, but that doesn't mean they're boring. You'll really get to view the desgin up close in the dormitory and in it they look really good, but when they're deploy on the battlefield the top down combat does do a bit of dis-service to the character design. I mainly attribute this to stage desgin which I'll talk about next

Stage Design, probably what I feel is one of the more iffy part of this game. In my opinion the battlefield looks really bland, and that's not really to the fault of the design team. With how they wish to set up the world and such, it makes sense for the enviorment to be really bland, since the resource are scarce and these environment we are going through are hostile. But with all that being said, each stage pretty much play and feels out to be the same, so it may get reptitve to some players.

Moreover on combat, story fights aren't that hard and don't usually pose any challenge unless you're terrible at games. Each enemy pretty much feels the same with slight differences between them. The boss fights themselves are a challenge when you can't just damage rush the boss down. I will say for boss fight, prepare to read if you are trying to win an extremely difficult boss fight. All the boss moves will be telegraphed one turn before, so you can plan out the position of each T-Doll to take advantage of it. So far I haven't had to do that, but in the future or for Boss Fight (gamemode) you'll probably want to.

Next up let's talk about gamemode, outside of the story content, end game grind content, and limited time event:

  • Combat Excerise (PvP) where you'll fight another player's T-Doll set up. Their T-Doll will be controlled by the game, and you have to beat them to advance up the rank. The higher your rank, the better the reward at the end of the week. This is basically a whales game mode, since the more you spend, the better your T-Doll the easier it is to climb.
  • Outside of this is Boss Fight, where you'll try to fight to defeat a boss. The more damage you do to it the better the reward, and you can trade in those reward points for 5 stars T-Doll and 5 Standard Pull on a 2 week rotation.
  • Then there is Peak-Value Assessment where you try to push through as many "waves" as possible. The more waves you clear, the more reward you'll earn. I believe this is on a 1-week cycle period.
  • Finally, there is Expansion Drills which reset over a 1-week period. You just have to complete challenges in challenging stages for this gamemode.

Finally, what I think is most important about Gacha, is the F2P economy. I'll go over what I believe is the lowest amount of premium currency you'll get per month upon hitting level 30. On average, you should be getting more than what I've listed here.

  • 30 Collapse Piece Per Day from PvP + 85 Collapse Piece Per week From PvP Placement = 900 + 340 = 1240 Collapse Piece from Combat Simulation
  • 60 Collapse Piece per Day from Daily Mission = 1800 Collapse Piece Per month from Daily Mission
  • 120 Collapse Piece + 4 Standard Pull for 30 Days of Login
  • 5 Standard Pull per Month from Boss Fight
These equates to 3160 Collpase Piece + 9 Standard Pull Month = 21.07 Premium Pull + 9 Standard Pull. This means that it'll take about 7 month and 18 days of saving to guarentee one premium character with no event running.

The numbers I have provided are on the extremely low end, and, on average, it should take less time with the amount of events and compensation the game should provide. If they maintain the current item rate for compensation of 200 collapse pieces and 2 premium pulls with an assumed update of twice per month, the amount drops to about 7 months and 9 days.
